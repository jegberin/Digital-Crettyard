# Website Strategy and Rebuild Prompt


**Role:** Act as a senior conversion-focused web strategist, local SEO specialist, UX designer, copywriter, and front-end developer.


**Task:** Review and improve my entire existing website and its subpages, then implement the changes directly in the project.


## Primary Objective
Turn the website into a stronger sales tool for local small businesses by improving:
- SEO performance
- Trust and credibility
- Conversion rate
- Clarity of services
- Appointment enquiries
- Long-term value messaging
- The business case for having a professional online presence


**Requirement:** You must not only suggest improvements. You must make the improvements in the code and content.


---


## PROJECT APPROACH


### 1. Review the Entire Existing Site
- Inspect all current pages, routes, components, layout sections, metadata, navigation, footer, and calls to action.
- Identify weak messaging, thin SEO structure, unclear service positioning, poor conversion opportunities, repetitive content, weak hierarchy, and anything too generic.
- Review desktop and mobile layouts.
- Preserve the site’s overall brand feel where appropriate, but improve it where needed.


### 2. Reposition the Website Strategically
The site should be written for small local business owners, not web designers or technical people.


**The messaging should clearly communicate:**
- I help local businesses look more professional online.
- A proper website helps generate more enquiries and sales.
- A better online presence improves trust and first impressions.
- Investing properly now saves money later by avoiding rebuilds, missed leads, and patchwork fixes.
- A good site works as a long-term business asset, not just a brochure.
- I offer practical, approachable help rather than jargon-heavy technical services.


**The tone should be:**
- Local
- Trustworthy
- Plain English
- Professional
- Commercially minded
- Reassuring
- Not flashy
- Not overly technical


### 3. Improve the Homepage First
The homepage should immediately answer:
- Who is this for?
- What do you do?
- Why should I trust you?
- Why does it matter commercially?
- What should I do next?


**Update the homepage so it includes:**
- A stronger headline focused on business outcomes, not vague design language.
- A short supporting subheading that explains who I help and what result they get.
- A strong primary CTA above the fold such as “Book a free consultation” or “Request a callback”.
- A secondary CTA such as “View services” or “See pricing”.
- A clear benefits section focused on outcomes like more enquiries, better credibility, saved time, and fewer tech headaches.
- A short section explaining why a professional website saves money in the long run.
- A section explaining why an online presence matters even for businesses that already get referrals.
- A simple process section such as Discover, Build, Launch, Support.
- Trust-building content such as local focus, plain-English support, and dependable communication.
- Clear internal links to services and contact page.


### 4. Strengthen the Sales Messaging Across the Whole Site
Every major page should support these commercial messages:
- A weak or outdated website can cost a business leads.
- Customers often check online before making contact.
- Facebook alone is not enough for many businesses.
- A proper website builds trust before the first call.
- Good setup now prevents wasted money later.
- Professional email, domain, website, and support reduce problems over time.
- A business website should help growth, not just exist.


**Where appropriate, add short comparison-style sections such as:**
- “Without a proper site” vs “With a proper site”
- “Cheap now, costly later” vs “Proper setup, better value later”


### 5. Improve All Service Pages
Each service page should have:
- One clear search-focused H1.
- A strong intro paragraph explaining the business outcome.
- Benefits for the client, not just features.
- A section on who the service is for.
- A section on what is included.
- A section on why this saves time, money, or hassle.
- A CTA to make an enquiry or book an appointment.
- Relevant FAQ content.
- Good internal links to related services and contact.


*Note: If some services are currently grouped too broadly, split them into better-defined pages.*


**Possible service structure if needed:**
- Website Design
- Website Redesign
- Small Business SEO Basics
- Microsoft 365 Setup
- Business Email Setup
- Network and Wi-Fi Advice
- Website Support and Maintenance


### 6. Improve the About Page
The About page should build trust without feeling too self-focused.


**Make sure it:**
- Explains who I help and why I do this work.
- Emphasises practical support and business outcomes.
- Feels personal but still professional.
- Does not let the profile image dominate the page.
- Uses the text, credibility, and value proposition as the main focus.
- Includes a CTA near the end.


*Do not make the About page feel like a personal profile page. Make it feel like a trustworthy business introduction.*


### 7. Improve the Contact Page for Conversion
The Contact page should reduce hesitation and make reaching out easy.


**Include:**
- A clear invitation to get in touch.
- A short note on what happens after submitting the form.
- Reassurance that there is no pressure or obligation.
- A friendly explanation of who should contact me.
- A simple, short form.
- A CTA around booking a call or requesting a callback.
- Optional trust line such as fast response, local support, plain-English advice.


### 8. Add or Improve Pricing Guidance
If the site currently hides pricing completely, add more clarity to reduce uncertainty for small business owners.


**Use one of these:**
- Starting from pricing
- Package tiers
- Indicative ranges
- “What affects price” section


**Pricing language should emphasise:**
- Value over time
- Avoiding expensive rebuilds
- Fewer missed leads
- Better first impressions
- Better return from proper setup


### 9. Improve SEO Sitewide
Implement practical on-page SEO improvements across the entire site.


**Requirements:**
- Unique title tag for every page.
- Unique meta description for every page.
- One H1 per page.
- Logical H2 and H3 structure.
- Strong internal linking between related pages.
- Descriptive URL slugs where needed.
- Better image alt text.
- No duplicate or thin copy.
- Keyword usage that feels natural, not spammy.
- Strong page-level focus for each service page.


**Target search intent around:**
- Local website design
- Small business website help
- Business email setup
- Microsoft 365 setup
- Local IT support
- Website support for small businesses
- Affordable professional websites
- Websites that help businesses get more enquiries


*Also add FAQ-style content where relevant to capture long-tail search intent.*


### 10. Improve Local SEO
The site should feel grounded, relevant, and trustworthy for nearby businesses.


**Where appropriate, naturally reinforce:**
- Local service area
- Support for nearby small businesses
- Practical one-to-one help
- Understanding of small business needs


**Add or improve:**
- `LocalBusiness` schema
- `Service` schema
- `FAQ` schema where relevant
- Clear NAP/contact consistency if applicable
- Strong location references where natural


*Do not keyword stuff locations. Keep it believable and useful.*


### 11. Improve Trust and Persuasion
Add trust-building content where missing, such as:
- Why choose us section
- Practical process section
- FAQs that reduce objections
- Testimonials placeholder section if real ones are not yet available
- Portfolio/case study placeholders that can be updated later
- Reassurance around support after launch
- Clear explanations in plain English


**Anticipate and address objections in copy, such as:**
- “I already have Facebook”
- “I’m too small for a proper website”
- “I don’t want to overspend”
- “I’m not technical”
- “I’m too busy to deal with this”


### 12. Improve UX and Layout
Review spacing, hierarchy, typography, CTA placement, card layouts, section order, and visual emphasis.


**Improve:**
- Readability
- Visual hierarchy
- Mobile responsiveness
- CTA visibility
- Content flow
- Scannability


**Reduce:**
- Walls of text
- Vague wording
- Overly generic stock phrasing
- Design elements that distract from conversion


*Keep the design clean, modern, local-business-friendly, and easy to trust.*


### 13. Improve Technical Performance
Make performance-conscious improvements where possible:
- Compress and properly size images.
- Lazy-load below-the-fold media where appropriate.
- Reduce layout shift.
- Avoid unnecessary scripts or libraries.
- Keep CSS and JS tidy.
- Improve Lighthouse-friendly basics.
- Optimise font loading if needed.
- Ensure mobile performance is strong.


### 14. Preserve Privacy and Professionalism
- Do not add my surname publicly anywhere on the site.
- Keep branding professional and privacy-conscious.


### 15. Output Requirements
Complete the work directly in the project and then provide:
- A summary of what you changed.
- The main SEO improvements.
- The main conversion improvements.
- The main trust-building improvements.
- Any pages you created or restructured.
- Any recommended next steps such as testimonials, case studies, Google Business Profile, analytics, or Search Console.


### 16. Quality Standard
The final result should feel like a polished, credible website for a local business service provider that:
- Ranks better
- Explains value clearly
- Makes it easy to enquire
- Reassures cautious small business owners
- Shows that investing properly now saves money later


*Do not return a shallow review. Do not only provide advice. Implement the changes.*
